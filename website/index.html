Frequently Asked Questions

What platforms are supported?

Microsoft Store (WACK-compliant), Windows desktop .ico, macOS, Android (Play Store), iOS (App Store), Chrome Extension, Favicon, Social/OG images, and Flatpak. Each platform includes all required icon sizes and store graphics.

Is my image uploaded to a server?

No. All processing happens locally in your browser using the Canvas API. Your image never leaves your device.

What image size should I use?

At least 1024x1024 for best results, since some assets (like iOS App Store icons) require that resolution. PNG is preferred for transparency.

ZIP or folder?

Use the "Save as ZIP" toggle. When on, you get a single ZIP. When off, the assets are written into a folder you choose (supported in Chrome/Edge; other browsers fall back to a ZIP).

What is the difference between Letterbox and Crop?

Letterbox (contain) fits your whole image inside the target, adding transparent padding if the aspect ratio differs. Crop (cover) fills the target, trimming edges. For square icons they look the same.

How do Windows Store badge logos work?

Badge logos must pass WACK: all visible pixels must be pure white on a transparent background. The tool removes the background automatically using dominant-color detection and Otsu thresholding, then forces the foreground to white.

What is the "Flutter naming convention" option?

When enabled, iOS and macOS icons are exported with the filenames Flutter expects (e.g. Icon-App-60x60@2x.png), so you can drop them straight into a Flutter project.
